# Slide 1 — Title

**Dynamic Equity: Real-time, contribution-based ownership for teams**
One-liner: *“Points earned → ownership earned. Transparent. Auditable. Fair.”*
Speaker note: 30-second hook: problem, one-line solution, and why Stacks/Clarity is the secure blockchain choice.

---

# Slide 2 — The Problem

* Traditional equity allocations are static, negotiated once and rarely revisited.
* Early founders or powerful negotiators can hold outsized claims despite minimal ongoing contribution.
* New high-impact contributors (hire, contractor, late-founder) are under-rewarded.
* Lack of transparent, trustable measurement causes churn, disputes, and demotivation.
  Speaker note: Tell a short example: “A developer joins later, ships major product — but receives negligible equity relative to impact.”

---

# Slide 3 — The Need

* A way to **measure contribution** reliably and transparently.
* A **mechanism to convert contribution into ownership** without repeated legal renegotiation.
* Strong on-chain audit trail so teams can verify and trust the math.
* A system that works for startups, DAOs, enterprises, and communities.
  Speaker note: Emphasize psychological benefits — fairness increases retention and productivity.

---

# Slide 4 — Our Solution

**A platform that:**

1. Records contributions as verifiable events (evidence URL, signed claim).
2. Runs an AI-powered scoring engine to assign points (with human override).
3. Stores points on Stacks/Clarity (authoritative ledger) and exposes live equity % = user_points / total_points.
4. Provides leaderboards, per-contribution pages (date, points, evidence), audit logs, and governance/dispute tools.
   Speaker note: Walk through the flow: submit → score → approve → on-chain update → live equity. Mention admin overrides & audit trail.

# Slide 7 — Business Model & Scalability

**Revenue streams**

1. SaaS subscription (tiered): startups & orgs pay per-org/month for admin tools, analytics, governance.
2. Onboarding & legal packaging: paid templates + legal integration (for token→legal share wrappers).
3. Enterprise integrations & connectors (GitHub, Stripe, Jira) — premium connectors.
4. Transaction fees (opt-in) for on-chain relayer/batching service or for issuing legal-wrapped shares.
5. Marketplace (later): secondary liquidity / buybacks / tokenized share custody services (permissioned).

**Scalability**

* Start with a single-org SaaS model (low friction), then expand into DAOs, enterprises, and licensed asset-tokenization services.
* Multi-tenant architecture, caching leaderboards, and batched on-chain updates minimize gas & latency.
  Speaker note: Emphasize recurring revenue and upsell paths (AI scorer tuning, legal services).

# Slide 9 — Point(s) of Differentiation (why we win)

1. **Contribution-first ownership (points → equity)** — simple, auditable formula everyone understands.
2. **On-chain authority on Stacks/Clarity** — Bitcoin-anchored security and a decidable language for predictable financial logic. ([clarity-lang.org][3])
3. **AI-assisted scoring with human-in-the-loop** — scalable, explainable suggestions + admin override to reduce bias and speed approvals.
4. **Per-contribution transparency** — every point has metadata: date, evidence, who approved, and on-chain proof. This reduces disputes.
5. **Composability** — works for startups, DAOs, communities, and enterprises; can integrate with legal wrappers (token → legal share) for regulated markets.
